Exploration 5.3.10.

In this activity, we'll seek to understand the following claim:

Every planar graph \(G\) with no loops or multiple edges must contain at least one vertex of degree less than or equal to 5.
We'll approach this by assuming the conclusion is false and reaching a contradiction.

  • To assume the conclusion is false, we should assume that each vertex of \(G\) has degree at least what?

  • Let \(v\) denote the number of vertices in the graph. Since every edge has two ends, the number of edges \(e\) in the graph is \(e =6v/2 = 3v\text{.}\) Why is this a problem?

  • What if there are vertices of degree larger than 6?

  • Why can we conclude that every planar graph with no loops or multiple edges must have a vertex of degree 5 or less?
