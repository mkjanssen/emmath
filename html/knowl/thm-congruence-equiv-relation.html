Theorem 6.1.7.

Let \(m\gt 1\) be an integer, and suppose \(a,b,c\) are integers.

  • Congruence is reflexive: \(a\equiv a\mod m\)

  • Congruence is symmetric: If \(a\equiv b\mod m\text{,}\) then \(b\equiv a\mod m\text{.}\)

  • Congruence is transitive: If \(a\equiv b\mod m\) and \(b\equiv c\mod m\text{,}\) then \(a\equiv c\mod m\text{.}\)
